Cleveland Compliance: What You Need to Know

Cleveland's lead ordinance has real consequences. Properties built before 1978 cannot be leased without Lead-Safe Certification. Here's how we keep your portfolio compliant.

Lead-Safe Certification Is Not Optional

Every pre-1978 Cleveland rental needs a passing risk assessment and clearance exam before you can sign a lease. Non-compliance means fines, court dates, and vacant units generating zero revenue.

The 20-Year Exemption: Long-Term Savings

Full abatement qualifies your property for a 20-year exemption from recurring certification. For large portfolios, this eliminates thousands in repeat inspection costs. We manage the full process.

Questions Property Managers Ask

How do you handle multi-unit buildings?
We schedule all units in a single visit when possible, coordinate tenant access, and deliver one consolidated report with per-unit breakdowns. One invoice, one point of contact. Buildings with 5+ units get volume pricing automatically.
Do tenants need to leave during the inspection?
No. Normal occupancy is fine during the assessment. We work around furniture and daily routines with minimal disruption. For clearance exams after abatement, the unit should be vacant and cleaned before we arrive.
How quickly can I get a report?
Standard turnaround is 10–14 days from site visit to completed report. Rush options are available when you have a lease deadline or city submission date approaching. Lab processing takes 5–7 business days.
What happens if hazards are found?
You get a prioritized action plan, not just a pass/fail result. Options range from interim controls (cleaning, targeted repairs) to full abatement. We connect you with licensed contractors and help identify funding through city and county assistance programs.
How much does it cost?
Single-family risk assessments typically range from $550–$650. Multi-unit properties receive volume pricing that decreases per-unit as your portfolio grows. Contact us for an exact quote—we can usually provide one within a few hours.
Can you help with financial assistance programs?
Absolutely. We help clients navigate the Lead Safe Home Fund, City of Cleveland LHC grants, and Cuyahoga County programs. Many property owners qualify for assistance that covers most or all of their compliance costs.
