Cleveland-Specific Compliance

Lead-Safe Certification Required

Cleveland rental properties built before 1978 must obtain Lead-Safe Certification before leasing. This requires a passing lead risk assessment and clearance exam.

20-Year Exemption Pathway

Properties that complete full lead-based paint abatement can qualify for a 20-year exemption from routine re-certification. We guide the inspection, abatement coordination, and final clearance.

Typical Timeline

Standard risk assessment: 10–14 days from site visit to report. Rush options available for faster turnaround when needed.

Rental Licensing Connection

Lead-Safe Certification is required for rental registration renewal in Cleveland. We provide documentation formatted for seamless submission to the city.

Common Questions

How long does an assessment take?
The on-site visit typically takes 1–2 hours per unit. We collect dust wipes, soil samples (if applicable), and document painted surfaces. Lab processing takes 5–7 business days for standard turnaround.
Do tenants need to leave?
No. Normal occupancy is fine during the assessment. We work around furniture and daily routines. For clearance exams after abatement, the unit should be vacant and cleaned before we arrive.
What if we find hazards?
We provide a clear, prioritized action plan. Options range from simple interim controls (cleaning, repairs, repainting) to full abatement. We connect you with licensed contractors and help identify funding opportunities.
How much does it cost?
Single-family risk assessments typically range from $550–$650, depending on property size, condition, renovation history, and accessibility. Multi-family properties receive volume pricing. Contact us for an exact quote.
